One Lock
INSECURE

Just a password. That's it.

This used to work.

Before the internet. Before AI.

The Problem

One password. That's all that stands between your accounts and someone else.

AI can try millions of passwords per second. It learns from billions of leaked passwords. It knows the patterns you use.

[IMAGE: Password cracking visualization]
Before Internet
1FA - One password was enough. Simple times.
Now
AI breaks passwords in seconds. One lock isn't enough.

One lock? That's not security. That's hope.

2
Two Locks
BETTER

Password + Phone

This was the answer.

For the internet age. But times changed.

Two locks. Password plus your phone. Even if someone steals your password, they need your phone too.

[IMAGE: 2FA process diagram]

But here's what AI can do now:

SIM Swapping: They call your phone company. They pretend to be you. They transfer your number to their phone.

Voice Cloning: AI clones your voice from videos. It sounds exactly like you. The phone company believes it.

[IMAGE: SIM swap attack flow]
"Two locks worked. Until AI learned how to pick the second one."

Two locks? Better. But not enough. Not anymore.

So what do you do?

Start simple. Build from there.

Start Here

1. Use a password manager

2. Enable 2FA (authenticator app, not SMS)

3. Use biometrics on your phone

Level Up

1. Get a YubiKey

2. Enable 3FA on important accounts

3. Protect your phone number with a PIN
